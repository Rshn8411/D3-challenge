Poverty and Access to Healthcare

Poverty and the Uninsured:

National poverty levels for 2020 for a household of four persons is $25,750. As we can see from the graph above states with greater poverty are certainly less likely to have access to healthcare compared to states with less poverty. Texas has the highest percent of individuals without healthcare, but does not have the greatest percentage of families in poverty. Similarly, Mississippi has the highest percentage of families in poverty but is not at the very top for percentage lacking healthcare. Thus, we might assume a correlation between these two variables but certainly there are other mitigating factors.
